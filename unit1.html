1.7 - Where digital project involves the use of personal data then the GDPR regulations apply especially if the digital project involves things like IP addresses, mobile device ID's, browser fingerprints, RFID tags, MAC addresses, cookies, user account IDs and any other form of system -generated data which identifies a natural person.
Where a digital project involves the use of Cookies then it is important to let people/my client know what the cookies do and why. This is covered by Regulation 6 of GDPR. Regulation 6 GDPR applies to anyone working on a digital project where information is stored on a user's device or gains access to information on a user's device.
Articles 13 & 14 of GDPR specifies that individuals/my clients have the right to be informed about what is done with their data . This is important when working on a Digital Project .When working on a Digital Project which involves collecting personal data from Individuals - then I need to give my Clients the name and contact number of the organisation, I work for, contact details of the DPO , explain the purposes of the processing, and the lawful basis for the processing. I also need to provide the individual/my client with privacy information . Usually the privacy notification would be on my website as the Digital Content technician /developer carrying out the digital job/task.
Also the PECR (Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations 2003 sometimes apply alongside the GDPR. These Regulations address new risks to businesses and users engaged in digital projects. The PECR applies to digital companies engaged in marketing by phone, email, text or fax. The rules also apply where a digital content company uses Cookies, or similar technology on its website, or where a digital directory is being provided.
GPPR and PECR impact on the development work flow of digital projects. When working on a Digital Project, I will need to consider whether I need to encrypt the data, anonymise the Data and I would also need to put technical and security measures in place to protect my clients Data.
I am also aware that my Client has the right to exercise his or her Subject Access Rights relating to Data Portability, to having their Data erased, and also to having the right to be forgotten. These are covered in the GDPR.
